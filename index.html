The Nagoya Protocol on Access and Benefit Sharing

The Protocol is a framework for how to conduct research and manage collections and associated information.

It is focused on helping people realize the value biodiversity has and helping people access and receive fair benefits.

Read More




The Nagoya Protocol Aims To


Goal 1

Become more aware of the values of biodiversity and the knowledge people have developed to understand and use it. Consider what and who has access to biodiversity resources and associated knowledge, and what are immediate and long-term benefits that can stem from these.

Goal 2

Obtain Prior Informed Consent (PIC). Before accessing genetic resources and/or traditional knowledge, articulate the intended purpose of your work and its potential benefits. Seek competent national and local authorities to give PIC.

Goal 3

Develop mutually agreed terms (MAT). This includes a framework for how to share benefits and access to data, results or collections, whether there are any restrictions and whether certain situations may require renegotiation of the MAT.

The Nagoya Protocol Education Portal


How the Nagoya Protocol was developed, what it covers, and how people are to follow it, are not familiar to all of us, and the policies are still evolving.

This is a learning portal to help anyone doing science research, education, and collections management learn and promote Nagoya compliance.

This applies to both international and national work, and applies to future research planning as well as historical research resources.

We welcome you to our learning community.
